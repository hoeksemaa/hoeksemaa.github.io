## Learn faster and deeper
I learned a massive volume of content this week but was slower than I could've been. Here are several new ideas to implement:
- **Use Claude in "Feynman Mode"** (technique invented by Josh): prompt Claude to use the feynman technique. Force myself to explain key concepts to Claude until I believe I have sufficient understanding. In a world with Claude Code, my own understanding of concepts and ability to perform/justify system design is more important than implementing the code myself.
- **Notice animal-like panic; correct**: Mostly comes from class deadlines for work when I feel like I'm behind. 1) [intelectual justification following...] This is silly because the class isn't about assignments and deadlines, it's about maximum technical progress. Worrying about deadlines is dumb. 2) [emotional control...] I suspect this panic is anti-productive. Hormetic stress is real; little stress and fear is helpful to motivate, but panic that clouds my mind isn't. When I notice I am panicking, the first step is to stop panicking. Remind myself that I'm not physically in danger; allow myself to relax again. Remind myself of the motivations for working hard and doing this bootcamp.
- **Break work into smaller pieces**: Understand the dataflows between pieces before you begin. Add the library make the MVP functional piece; nothing else. commit that. Build the next small piece; nothing else. commit that. etc.
